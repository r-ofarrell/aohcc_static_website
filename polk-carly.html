Carly Polk, LPC

Carly is a Licensed Professional Counselor in the state of South Carolina. She obtained her Masters of Arts degree in Clinical Counseling from the Citadel Graduate College in 2019 and a Bachelors of Science Degree in Psychology with a minor in Sociology from Clemson University in 2016.

Upon graduating with her Masters degree in Clinical Counseling, Carly went straight into working at a child advocacy center as a Child and Family Therapist and Forensic Interviewer. During her time at the child advocacy center, Carly implemented a variety of trauma treatments with children, adolescents, and adults. In 2023 Carly transitioned to the Medical University of South Carolinaâ€™s National Crime Victim Research and Treatment Center where she worked with adolescents with trauma and substance use disorders while also supporting their families. Carly primarily operates from a cognitive behavioral lens and has been trained in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Trauma Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT), Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT), Cognitive Behavioral Therapy with Problematic Sexual Behaviors for Adolescents (CBT-PSB-A), Written Exposure Therapy (WET), and Risk Reduction Through Family Therapy (RRFT). Carly also has experience working with children on the autism spectrum, individuals with depression, anxiety, body image and self-esteem related issues, and adjustment related difficulties.

During her free time Carly enjoys hanging out with her family and friends and reading!
